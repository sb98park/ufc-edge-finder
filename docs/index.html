How the Model Works
📊
Elo + Stats Blend
Blends Elo (built from recorded fight history, when it exists) with a stats-based rating from career win rate, finish rate, experience, and reach.
🥋
Style Matchup — "Styles Make Fights"
Takedown accuracy vs. the opponent's takedown defense (or control time percentage, when populated — a fighter who goes 1-for-5 on takedowns but holds 4 minutes of control is more effective than raw accuracy implies), striking accuracy and volume differential (SLpM minus SApM, when populated), and durability all shift the base rating gap.
📉
Age Cliff by Weight Class
The decline threshold varies a lot by division — Flyweight/Bantamweight/Featherweight/Strawweight start seeing a real decline past 35, while Heavyweight and Light Heavyweight often sustain into the late 30s. A flat age penalty across all divisions would be wrong in both directions.
⏱️
Layoff / Ring Rust vs. Quick Return
Fighters back after more than a year away take a ring-rust penalty; fighters back too soon after being finished (under ~6 months) take a separate, opposite-direction penalty. The layoff weight was revised down sharply after backtesting against 30 years of fight history showed the original penalty was sized far too aggressively — the current weight is the strongest the historical evidence actually supports.
🔥
Recent Form (Backtest-Validated)
Each fighter's last three results, weighted by how recent they are (a win last month counts more than a win 18 months ago). Both the three-fight window and the weight were selected on pre-2019 historical fights and confirmed on held-out 2019+ fights — one of the few adjustments fitted to evidence rather than hand-tuned.
🤜
Stance Matchup
Southpaw or switch fighters get a modest edge against pure-orthodox opponents — the classic "unfamiliar look" advantage. Two fighters sharing a stance (including two southpaws) is neutral, since neither has the familiarity edge.
🐍
Submission Threat
A fighter's rate of finishing wins by submission, as a distinct skill from raw wrestling stats — a fighter can have modest takedown numbers but a live submission threat off scrambles and clinch entries, which takedown accuracy alone misses.
🔀
Weight Class Change
Moving up a division (facing bigger opponents) carries a penalty per division jumped; moving down earns a small bonus. Detected automatically from each fighter's tracked division history against the division this specific fight is booked at.
📏
Height Advantage
A small edge per inch of height difference — deliberately small, because reach (which tracks height closely) already carries weight in the base rating, and counting the same physical edge twice would overstate it.
🚨
Short Notice
Fighters who accept a bout on short notice — a late replacement without a full training camp — win measurably less often; published analyses of UFC short-notice outcomes put the drop at roughly 10 percentage points, which is the penalty applied here. Flagged per fighter when a late booking is confirmed, and cleared automatically once that fight happens, so a one-booking fact never bleeds into the fighter's next matchup.
⚖️
Missed Weight
A documented red flag for a rushed or broken camp — factored in when known, though real weigh-in history isn't fully populated for the roster yet (see limitations below).
🎯
Method of Victory
Prior-informed blend — starts at the divisional average rate for that method (heavyweight fights have an inherently higher baseline KO/TKO rate than strawweight, which leans toward decisions), shifts toward the fighter's own career tendency (weighted by experience), then further incorporates the specific opponent's demonstrated vulnerability.
🔔
Total Rounds
Blends combined finish rate with first-round finish percentage. Main events get 5-round-calibrated lines (3.5/4.5) instead of 3-round lines (1.5/2.5), since there's simply more scheduled fight to cover.
📈
Line Movement
Each refresh is compared against saved history to show real price movement and render an actual chart. Honest limitation: this tracks price only, not bet volume or who's behind a move — true "sharp money" detection needs handle/ticket-count data no free source provides, so this is labeled "notable movement," not a sharp-money claim.
💰
Suggested Stake
Tenth-Kelly, hard-capped at 5% of bankroll — and sized from a probability that's deliberately shrunk most of the way toward the market's own de-vigged price, not the raw model number. Backtesting showed the model's standalone probabilities carry real but modest signal, while sportsbook closing lines are among the sharpest predictors in sports — so when the model and the market disagree hugely, the stake respects that the market is usually the one that's right. Practical effect: big claimed edges get meaningfully smaller stakes than raw Kelly would suggest, and small edges (under roughly 5 points) stake zero, because after the shrink they no longer clear the vig. That's the system being deliberately humble, not broken.
🎢
Closing Line Value (CLV)
The moneyline price on the model's favorite the FIRST time it was logged, compared against the last known price before the fight. If the market moved toward the model's side by fight night (the price shortened), that's a "beat" — a real signal the model saw value before the market caught up, independent of whether the pick actually won. This is why CLV is tracked separately from raw accuracy: a model can lose a coinflip fight while still having correctly read where the market was headed, and that skill is what CLV isolates.
🔬
Historical Upset Analysis
Separately from live tracking, the rating engine is walked back through its full fight history in chronological order — replaying each fight using only the ratings as they stood the moment before, no future information — to see how favorites at each confidence level actually lost when they lost: close decision, or getting finished. This is diagnostic, not automatic: a pattern here is a reason to investigate a specific adjustment, not an instruction that silently reweights anything on its own.
🧪
Walk-Forward Backtest
The rating backbone is validated against 30 years of real fight history — nearly 3,000 strictly out-of-sample predictions, each made only from fights that happened before it, never retrodicted. This is also what keeps the adjustment weights honest: it's how the layoff penalty got cut to a third of its original size, how the recent-form window was fitted rather than guessed, and how a proposed strength-of-schedule factor got rejected entirely (it looked good in-sample and fell apart on held-out data — the exact failure the backtest exists to catch). Factors that can't be validated this way, like short notice, are labeled by what actually backs them instead of being presented as fitted.
⚠️ Honest data limitations: strike-volume (SLpM/SApM), control-time percentage, and missed-weight history are new schema fields the model is built to use, but they're not populated for the full roster yet — those specific adjustments quietly no-op (rather than guess) until real data backs them.
